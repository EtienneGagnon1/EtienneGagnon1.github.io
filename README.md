# Welcome to my GitHub Pages site

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. Student in Politics at Princeton University. 

I conduct research in Political Economy and Comparative Politics, with a focus on Japanese politics. My work appears in *The Journal of Politics* and *PS: Political Science & Politics*. My current research relates spatial economic inequality, migration and urbanization to electoral outcomes and collective action. Specifically, in my dissertation, I relate the long-run depopulation of the Japanese countryside and place-based redistributive policies to the LDP's continued electoral success. In additional papers, I relate economic changes brought by the opening of the Japanese economy and the subsequent development of the Japanese State to collective action and the expansion of suffrage in the 1920s. I also engage in additional research on Japanese political behavior, specifically the role of localism and elitism in Japanese politics. 

My main methodological interest lies in structural estimation and applications of models of Urban and Regional Economics to Political Science. At Princeton, I have served as a teaching assistant in Political Economy classes, witha focus on formal modelling. 

Before Princeton, I completed a Master's degree in Interdisciplinary Information Sciences at the University of Tokyo under the Japanese Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology (MEXT)'s scholarship program. Prior to this I was a Master's student in Political Science at McGill University. I received my Bsc in Political Science from the University of Montreal.
